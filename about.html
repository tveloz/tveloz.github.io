My Journey

I have always been fascinated by fundamental questions about the nature of reality, the meaning of life, and our purpose. With dual bachelor's degrees in Physics and Mathematics, followed by a master's in Computer Science, I have spent years pursuing these questions in interdisciplinary contexts. Today, my research spans cognitive science, complex systems, and artificial intelligence, focusing on the emergence of purpose in dynamic systems.

My work has taken me to international research centers and collaborations with leading scholars in the fields of quantum cognition, cybernetics, and complex systems. I founded the DICTA foundation to foster interdisciplinary development and international collaboration.
